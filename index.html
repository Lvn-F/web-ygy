GreenTech

Green Technology is a branch of technology that focuses on the development and application of products, systems and processes that use resources efficiently, produce minimal environmental impact, promote long-term sustainability, reduce greenhouse gas emissions and pollution.

Why Greentech Matter ?

By harnessing clean energy sources, reducing emissions, conserving resources, and promoting eco-friendly innovation, greentech helps protect the planet while supporting economic growth and improving quality of life. It plays a vital role in transitioning industries and societies toward a lo-carbon future, ensuring that environmental preservation and technological advancement go hand in hand for the benefit of current and future generations.

Gerakan Greentech

Solar Panel

A solar panel is a device used to convert sunlight into electrical energy using the principle of the photovoltaic effect. It consists of solar cells (usually made from semiconductor materials like silicon) that capture sunlight and generate direct current (DC) electricity.

Electric Vehicle

An electric vehicle (EV) is a vehicle powered by an electric motor and obtains its energy from a rechargeable battery, rather than from an internal combustion engine that uses gasoline or diesel.

LED light

LED (Light Emitting Diode) is an electronic component that emits light when an electric current flows through it. LED technology is used for lighting because it is more energy-efficient, long-lasting, and environmentally friendly compared to incandescent or fluorescent lamps.

Greentech History

Greentech, or green technology, has evolved from early conservation ideas in the 1800s to a global movement for sustainable innovation. Sparked by rising awareness of environmental damage during the Industrial Revolution and amplified by events like the 1970 Earth Day and Rachel Carson's Silent Spring, the movement gained momentum with policies like the Clean Air Act and institutions like the EPA. In the late 20th century, renewable energy, green architecture, and recycling emerged, leading to the modern greentech era marked by solar, wind, electric vehicles, and smart infrastructure. Key global agreements like the Kyoto Protocol and Paris Agreement drove cooperation, while recent advances such as AI for climate, green hydrogen, and circular economy practices are shaping a net-zero future
